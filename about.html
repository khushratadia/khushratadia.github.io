About Me

Mechanical engineering student with professional experience in automotive and robotics industries

I am a Mechanical Engineering student at the University of Washington, graduating in June 2027, with professional experience spanning the automotive and robotics industries. My focus areas include mechanical design, simulation, additive manufacturing, and subsystem integration.

Over the past few years, I have gained hands-on industry experience at Mahindra & Mahindra Ltd., where I analyzed over 100 hours of real-world diesel engine test data, simulated accessory performance with GT-SUITE, and redesigned components in CATIA V5 to improve efficiency across powertrain systems. Prior to this, I interned at CEAT Tires Ltd. in Materials & R&D, where I tested and optimized rubber compounds to enhance durability and manufacturing consistency.

At the University of Washington, I serve as a Mechanical Systems Engineer and Spectrometer Subsystem Lead in Husky Robotics, contributing to the University Rover Challenge. I led the design and manufacture of an automated cuvette transfer system, integrating stepper motor control with rack-and-pinion mechanics and magnetic stabilization to ensure reliable, spill-free sample analysis in competition environments.

Beyond team projects, I independently designed and manufactured a fully 3D-printed mechanical wall clock featuring a Geneva mechanism and deadbeat escapement. This project merged traditional horology with modern CAD, motion analysis, and additive manufacturing practices, reinforcing my ability to translate theoretical design into functional prototypes.
